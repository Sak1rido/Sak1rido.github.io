I am an undergraduate student at School of Information Science and Technology in Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen, supervised by Prof. Yufei Jiang. I major in Communication Engineering and minor in Economics.

 My research interests are Signal processing, Wireless communications and AI for communications.

Research Experience
======
Rotation-Aided Single-LED Visible Light Positioning

Undergraduate Researcher, Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen

Advisor: Prof. Yufei Jiang

	Developed a visible-light positioning architecture that estimates receiver position using measurements collected at multiple terminal orientations with a single LED and a single photodetector.

	Conducted Monte Carlo simulations to evaluate the effects of receiver rotation, measurement noise, and algorithm parameters on positioning performance.

	Designed and implemented an experimental prototype, achieving a mean positioning error of 3.7 cm under the evaluated experimental setting.

	Contributed to manuscript preparation, theoretical modeling, simulation design, experimental validation, and result analysis.

Semi-Blind Timing Synchronization for DCO-OFDM Visible-Light Communications

Undergraduate Researcher, Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen

Advisor: Prof. Yufei Jiang

	Investigated semi-blind timing synchronization schemes based on DC bias and null subcarriers for DCO-OFDM visible-light communication systems, reducing dependence on dedicated training sequences.

	Built an end-to-end MATLAB simulation platform covering QAM modulation, Hermitian symmetry, DCO-OFDM modulation, DC biasing and clipping, LED channel modeling, AWGN, synchronization estimation, and BER evaluation.

	Verified through Monte Carlo simulations that the Null-Subcarrier-based method achieves synchronization-error performance comparable to training-sequence-based methods at high SNR, with BER approaching that under perfect synchronization.

	Deriving an analytical approximation for the timing-synchronization error probability of the null-subcarrier-based scheme.

Location-Prior-Aided Wi-Fi Channel Charting with Coarse User Position Labels under Unknown AP Deployment

Undergraduate Research Intern, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen

Advisor: Prof. Junting Chen

	Processed real-world indoor Wi-Fi measurements collected along three trajectories.

	Constructed trajectory-wise RSSI, channel-energy, and spatial-distribution maps to investigate AP coverage, signal continuity, and the spatial consistency of CSI features.

	Developing a location-prior-aided multidimensional scaling framework that combines CSI-derived neighborhood relationships with noisy user-position priors to recover a spatially consistent two-dimensional channel map under unknown AP locations and antenna configurations.
